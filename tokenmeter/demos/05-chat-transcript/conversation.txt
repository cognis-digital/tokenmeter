user: I have a Python list of dicts and I want to group them by the "team"
field. What's the cleanest way?

assistant: Use itertools.groupby after sorting, or collections.defaultdict for
unsorted input. defaultdict is simplest:

from collections import defaultdict
groups = defaultdict(list)
for row in rows:
    groups[row["team"]].append(row)

This is O(n) and does not require the input to be sorted.

user: Nice. Now I want the count per team, sorted from most to least.

assistant: Wrap it in Counter and use most_common:

from collections import Counter
counts = Counter(row["team"] for row in rows)
ranked = counts.most_common()   # list of (team, count), highest first

user: And if two teams tie on count, I want them alphabetical. Does most_common
do that?

assistant: No — most_common breaks ties by insertion order, not alphabetically.
Sort explicitly with a compound key:

ranked = sorted(counts.items(), key=lambda kv: (-kv[1], kv[0]))

The -kv[1] sorts by count descending, and kv[0] breaks ties A→Z.
